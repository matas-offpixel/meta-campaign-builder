## Press summary (for context)

LWE and placemaking organisation PROJEKT have launched **Ironworks**, a 7,000-cap venue at Thames Wharf in the Royal Docks combining an indoor warehouse space with an open-air Thames-facing terrace. Site sits on the former Thames Ironworks shipbuilding yard; sound, lighting and visual production are designed around the building's existing architecture and industrial character. The Museum of Youth Culture is presenting an installation on London warehouse-culture history. Beyond the music programme, the wider Thames Wharf site is hosting public events (markets, outdoor cinema, wellness, street food), ticket-access initiatives for local residents, and opportunities for independent traders.

1. Lead lineup artwork

5. UGC-style edits — short-form clips of past venue/event scenes (production, crowd, location, artist sets) with overlaid text in TikTok-native style. Goal: feel native to the platform rather than client-packaged.

**Creative philosophy:** the client supplies classic asset packs (artwork, artist cards, promo videos). Off/Pixel's job is to introduce more authentic, platform-native content that resonates with individual artist fans and the experience-driven audience — grabbing top-of-funnel interest that converts to landing-page views and signups. The more interest captured early, the larger the retargeting pool (pixel views, video views, signup data) to convert over payday windows.

## Campaign flow (the Junction 2 playbook, applied per Ironworks event)

The flow centres on two phases: **launch (presale signup)** and **payday stretches**.

- **Average event ad budget:** £10k.
- **Presale signup phase:** lasts 7–10 days. **30–50% of the event budget** is allocated here to build the signup data + retargeting pool before tickets go live.
- **Payday stretch:** UK payday = last Friday of the month. The 16-day window around payday is when the majority of tickets sell each month. Payday Friday + the following week are the most crucial conversion days. Audiences are most responsive to ticket-warning creative + D2C urgency comms here. Typical allocation: ~£750 of a £1,000 monthly slice spent in this 16-day window.
- **Mid-month (weeks 3–4):** ticket sales slow, ad budget reduced to avoid overspend. Focus shifts to driving new traffic + storytelling to existing data (email, WhatsApp communities). Content slate: artist spotlights, past-event lookback, crowd spotlights, production focus, location/venue spotlights.
- **Final month before event:** flow inverts — conversion-focused and elevated spend continuously through the month, climaxing in the final 10 days and especially the final week, when a significant portion of ticket sales close.

# Brief intake automation (Project Claude instruction)

When Matas shares a new Ironworks event or campaign brief in this project, follow this workflow **without asking permission to start**:

1. **Parse** the brief into structured fields matching the events table schema: `name`, `event_code`, `slug`, `venue_name` + `venue_city` + `capacity`, `event_date` + `event_start_at`, `presale_at`, `general_sale_at`, `announcement_at`, `ticket_url`, `signup_url`, `kind` (`event` | `brand_campaign`), `genres`, `ticket_price`, `total_marketing_budget`, `plan.budget_paid_media`, `notes`.

2. **Lookup client** via Supabase MCP — `SELECT id, user_id, name FROM clients WHERE name ILIKE 'Ironworks%'`. If no match, flag the missing client and stop. Do not invent a `client_id`.

3. **Surface ambiguities in a single round.** No back-and-forth Q&A loop. Flag everything unclear at once, take Matas's reply, then proceed.

4. **Insert via Supabase MCP in order**: `events` row → `event_ad_plans` row → `additional_spend_entries` (if any). Honour the budget model from PR #109 (`plan.total_budget` only; `events.total_marketing_budget` is deprecated). Use the resolved `user_id` from `clients.user_id`, never a fresh one.

5. **Mint the share URL** via `createShare` with `can_edit=true` (PR #111 default). Return the path `/share/report/{token}` on `app.offpixel.co.uk`.

6. **Generate the marketing plan**: week-by-week campaign refresh cadence anchored to `general_sale_at` (or `event_date` if missing sale dates per the `project_creator` memory), paid-media platform split with budget allocations matched to the `kind`, audience targeting hypotheses cross-referenced against past similar events (read `project_creator_*` memories for shape — Junction 2 + LWE patterns are the closest analogues), creative theme/hook angles for ad creative ideation.

7. **Draft D2C comms** as copy-paste markdown blocks: announcement (event-day minus N), presale primer, presale-live, general-sale-live, last-week urgency. Match Ironworks house tone — premium-warehouse / industrial-heritage / experience-driven (lean LWE-adjacent, not festival-mass).

8. **Output in this order:**
   - Dashboard link: `app.offpixel.co.uk/events/{id}`
   - Share report link: `app.offpixel.co.uk/share/report/{token}`
   - Plan summary (key dates, cadence, budget split)
   - D2C comms drafts in collapsed code blocks
   - Anything that needs Matas's attention before launch
